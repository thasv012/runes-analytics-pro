What are Runes?

Runes are a new tokenization protocol on the Bitcoin network, introduced through the BIP-428 proposal. They allow the creation of fungible tokens directly on the Bitcoin blockchain, without the need for sidechains or other security compromises.

Unlike other protocols such as BRC-20, Runes are designed to be more efficient, integrating directly with Bitcoin Core, leveraging the full security of the network and facilitating transactions at scale.

A Rune is represented as an 8-byte integer:
R(edic.ether.symbol.reserved)
